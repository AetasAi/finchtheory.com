## What Finch Theory does

Finch Theory unlocks commercial value that already exists inside businesses — through three services:

1. **Relationship Capital** — turning professional networks into a reliable source of introductions, referrals and new revenue
2. **Workforce Performance** — identifying and removing the hidden commercial cost of financial pressure in the workforce
3. **Fractional Growth Director** — senior commercial leadership on a retained or project basis

## Who it is for

SMEs and owner-managed businesses that are generating revenue, have genuine commercial potential, and need experienced outside thinking rather than a full-time overhead. The typical client has grown quickly on the strength of their people and relationships, and finds that commercial infrastructure — pipeline structure, network activation, workforce resilience — did not keep pace.

## Relationship Capital — detailed

Relationship capital is the aggregate commercial value embedded in professional relationships. Most businesses have accumulated years of relationships and manage almost none of that network as a commercial asset.

Finch Theory's Relationship Capital service:
- Maps the existing network with commercial intent
- Identifies referral nodes (5-15 contacts most positioned to generate introductions)
- Defines introduction triggers (specific circumstances when an introduction is timely)
- Sharpens the proposition so it travels (30-second articulation non-experts can repeat)
- Builds the reciprocity framework that sustains referral relationships
- Activates the architecture and measures commercial outcomes

Dedicated service page: https://finchtheory.com/relationship-capital.html

## Workforce Performance — detailed

Financial pressure is the most common workplace stressor in the UK. Around 1 in 4 employees is significantly financially stressed, costing employers £10.3bn annually in lost productivity. Different generations face different pressures:

- **Gen Z**: housing costs, cost-of-living, career uncertainty
- **Millennials**: mortgage rate shocks, childcare, pension shortfalls (avg £48k gap)
- **Gen X**: sandwich generation pressures, £112k average pension shortfall
- **Baby Boomers**: drawdown complexity, estate planning, long-term care (avg £98k liability)

## Fractional Growth Director — detailed

Most BD pipelines fail for three structural reasons: wrong contacts, unclear proposition, no system. A Fractional Growth Director fixes the structure before recommending a hire — providing pipeline strategy, opportunity qualification and commercial judgment on a retained or project basis.
